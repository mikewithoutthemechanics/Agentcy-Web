**Post 2 — Thought leadership:**
The biggest mistake I see SMEs make with technology?

Trying to build everything at once.

"We need a CRM, inventory system, booking platform, invoicing tool, and analytics dashboard. Can you build it all?"

No. And you shouldn't want us to.

The best software starts with one painful problem and solves it completely.

WaitUp didn't start as a full booking platform. It started as a simple tool that filled empty yoga class slots.

Once that worked, we added features.

Start with the pain. Solve it. Measure the result. Then decide what to build next.

That's how every successful software product was built.

## Medium Bio & First Article

**Medium username:** @agentcy (or @agentcy-ai)
**Bio:**

**First article draft — "Why Every SME Needs a Software Strategy (Not Another SaaS Subscription)":**

Every growing business hits the same wall: the tools that got you here won't get you there.

You started with a spreadsheet. Then a CRM. Then a booking tool. Then an invoicing app. Then a project manager. Before you know it, you're paying R15,000/month for 12 tools that don't talk to each other.

Your team spends half their day copying data between systems. Things slip through the cracks. Reporting is a manual nightmare.

Sound familiar?

Here's what we've learned building software for SMEs across South Africa:

**The problem isn't the tools. It's the gaps between them.**

A booking system that doesn't talk to your CRM means lost customers. A stock system disconnected from your purchasing means stockouts. A payroll tool that can't see your timesheets means errors.

Custom software doesn't mean "build everything from scratch." It means building the glue that makes your existing tools work as one system — and filling the gaps that no SaaS product can fill.

**What custom software actually looks like for an SME:**

Take a yoga studio chain we worked with. They had a booking app, a payment processor, a CRM, and WhatsApp for customer communication. Four tools. Zero integration.

We built one layer that connected all four. Bookings automatically update the CRM. No-shows trigger WhatsApp messages to waitlisted customers. Payments reconcile automatically. The owner gets a daily dashboard instead of four different logins.

Total cost: less than what they were spending on the four tools individually.

**The real question isn't "build vs buy." It's "where are the gaps?"**

Buy your email. Buy your accounting. Buy your video calls. Those are commodity tools.

But the thing that gives you a competitive edge — your unique workflow, your customer experience, your operational advantage — that's what you build.

That's custom software. And for SMEs, it's more accessible than ever.
